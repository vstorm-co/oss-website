This article by Philipp Schmid identifies two key capabilities that separate effective AI agents from poor ones: self-awareness (operational self-knowledge about constraints, tools, and uncertainty) and closing the loop (verifying work against external signals before responding). Production systems like Spotify's coding agent demonstrate scaffolded verification through independent verifiers and LLM judges, while DeepMind research on intrinsic self-critique shows promise for spontaneous verification without external signals. The author argues that as self-awareness becomes trained-in and loop-closing becomes default behavior, agents will evolve from tools requiring constant supervision into trusted collaborators capable of continuous learning across sessions.
